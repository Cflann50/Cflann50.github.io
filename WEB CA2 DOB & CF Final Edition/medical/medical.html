Medical Scans

We provide thorough examinations across various body areas, ensuring you receive comprehensive insights into your health.

Abdominal / Pelvic Ultrasound Scan €220

The scan examines the liver, pancreas, gallbladder, kidneys, aorta, and spleen in the upper abdomen and the bladder, uterus, endometrium and ovaries in the pelvis.

Abdominal/Renal scan €180

Visualises the organs and structures within the upper abdomen and the bladder and prostate (in males). Checks for a referral is RUQ pain, renal stones, bladder and kidney infections.

Female Pelvic Ultrasound Scan €180

Visualises the organs and structures within the female pelvic region. Insights into the health and condition of the uterus, endometrium, cervix, bladder, ovaries and adjacent pelvic structures.

Lumps & Bumps Ultrasound Scan €130

A comprehensive evaluation of the lump and adjacent structures to diagnose the nature of the lump, size, shape, and its vascularity. Ultrasound is the first-line method for soft-tissue masses evaluation.

Paediatric Hip Ultrasound Scan €150

Ultrasound of the neonatal hip plays a role in screening, diagnosis, and (monitoring of) treatment of baby hip problems. If the hip socket is too shallow, and the development of the femoral head is delayed.

Thyroid / Neck Ultrasound Scan €150

The thyroid and neck ultrasound produces images of the thyroid and the adjacent structures such as the salivary glands and lymph nodes. The scan uses warm gel on the front of the neck and takes ten minutes.

Paediatric Abdomen / Renal Ultrasound Scan €220

Abdominal and renal scans are common and the main reason for a referral is abdominal pain in children, frequency in going to the toilet to empty the bladder, bladder infections and constipation.

Calf Ultrasound Scan €150

A common muscle to tear in sporting activities. It is important to assess all the detailed musculoskeletal structures involved in the calf to limit the risk of re-injury and ensure a speedy recovery.

Hamstring Ultrasound Scan €130

The risk of re-injury is extremely high in hamstring injuries. It is extremely important that the strain is detected and graded to inform the rehabilitation period and reduce the risk of injury.
